Curated updates from FDA, MDCG, IMDRF, and international standards bodies — covering SaMD, cybersecurity, and AI/ML regulatory developments.

EU / MDCG AI/ML New April 2025
MDCG 2025-6 Published: Guidance on Medical Device AI Under MDR/IVDR and EU AI Act
The Medical Device Coordination Group released MDCG 2025-6, providing the most comprehensive guidance to date on how AI-enabled medical devices must comply with both EU MDR/IVDR and the EU AI Act simultaneously. The guidance addresses dual compliance architecture, data governance obligations, bias examination, transparency requirements, human oversight design, and cybersecurity testing for AI systems. Manufacturers with AI/ML medical devices targeting EU markets must review this document immediately.
Impact: High — Affects all AI/ML medical device CE marking strategies from 2025 forward

FDA Cybersecurity Final Guidance Sept 2023
FDA Finalizes Cybersecurity Premarket Guidance — SBOM Now Required
FDA issued its final guidance on cybersecurity in medical devices, replacing the 2014 draft. The guidance formally requires a machine-readable Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), threat model, cybersecurity architecture documentation, security testing evidence, and a coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy in all premarket submissions for cyber devices. This guidance implements the statutory requirements under Section 524B of the FD&C Act, giving FDA authority to refuse-to-file submissions lacking cybersecurity documentation.
Impact: Critical — All 510(k), PMA, and De Novo submissions for cyber devices must include SBOM and full cybersecurity package
→ Full Guidance
FDA AI/ML Draft Guidance April 2023
FDA Issues Draft Guidance on PCCP for AI/ML-Enabled Devices
FDA published draft guidance on Predetermined Change Control Plans (PCCPs) for AI/ML-enabled medical devices, providing detailed recommendations on the structure and content required. The guidance describes the three PCCP components: description of anticipated modifications, modification protocol specifying validation methodology, and impact assessment. PCCPs enable manufacturers to make pre-approved AI algorithm updates without submitting new 510(k) applications for each change — a critical pathway for iterative AI improvement.
Impact: High — Defines the regulatory pathway for all AI/ML medical devices requiring algorithm updates post-clearance
→ Draft Guidance
EU AI/ML In Force August 2024
EU AI Act Enters Into Force — Medical Device AI Classified as High-Risk
The EU Artificial Intelligence Act entered into force on August 1, 2024, with phased application periods. Medical device AI systems listed in Annex III are classified as high-risk, triggering mandatory compliance with Articles 9–15 and 72 — covering risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, accuracy/robustness, and post-market monitoring. The Act applies cumulatively with EU MDR/IVDR obligations, creating a dual-compliance requirement comprehensively addressed in MDCG 2025-6.
Impact: Critical — All AI-enabled medical devices for EU market require dual MDR + AI Act compliance strategy
→ EU AI Act
FDA SaMD Final Rule Feb 2024
FDA QMSR Final Rule: 21 CFR Part 820 Harmonized with ISO 13485
FDA published the final Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR), amending 21 CFR Part 820 to align with ISO 13485:2016. Effective February 2026, the QMSR replaces the legacy Quality System Regulation. Manufacturers currently certified to ISO 13485:2016 by an accredited certification body will substantially meet QMSR requirements. SaMD manufacturers should assess their QMS against the new regulation and update procedures for design controls, risk management integration, and software lifecycle documentation.
Impact: Moderate — SaMD manufacturers must update QMS documentation to align with QMSR by February 2026
→ QMSR Info
EU Cybersecurity In Effect Oct 2024
NIS2 Directive Implementation Deadline Passed — Healthcare Now "Essential"
The EU NIS2 Directive (2022/2555) implementation deadline of October 17, 2024 has passed. Healthcare organizations are classified as "essential entities" and must now comply with mandatory cybersecurity risk management measures and incident reporting. Significant cyber incidents must be reported to national competent authorities within 24 hours (early warning) and 72 hours (full report). Medical device manufacturers operating in the EU healthcare supply chain should assess their NIS2 exposure and align cybersecurity governance accordingly.
Impact: High — EU healthcare organizations and their supply chains face new mandatory cybersecurity and incident reporting obligations
→ NIS2 Text
IMDRF SaMD Guidance 2023
IMDRF Reaffirms SaMD Clinical Evaluation Framework — N41 Remains Foundational
IMDRF confirmed that the N41:2017 SaMD Clinical Evaluation framework remains the primary international reference for SaMD clinical evidence requirements. FDA, Health Canada, TGA, and MHRA all reference N41 in their national guidance and submission expectations. The three building blocks — valid clinical association, analytical validation, and clinical validation — continue to define the minimum evidentiary standard for SaMD submissions globally. IMDRF is monitoring AI/ML-specific clinical evaluation needs that may require updates to N41.
Impact: Informational — N41 framework confirmed as global standard; AI/ML update may follow
→ IMDRF N41
FDA / HC / MHRA AI/ML Joint Statement 2021 / Ongoing
GMLP Principles: FDA, Health Canada, and MHRA International Harmonization
The joint statement on Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) for medical device development, issued by FDA, Health Canada, and MHRA, represents the most significant international harmonization effort in AI medical device regulation. The 10 GMLP principles cover multi-disciplinary expertise, data management, training-test independence, representativeness, transparency, human-AI teaming, and post-market monitoring. Manufacturers targeting any of these three jurisdictions should treat GMLP compliance as a baseline requirement.
Impact: High — GMLP defines the minimum expected standard for AI/ML SaMD across three major jurisdictions
→ GMLP Principles
IEC Cybersecurity Published 2021
IEC 81001-5-1 Published: Security Standard for Health Software
IEC 81001-5-1:2021 established the first dedicated cybersecurity engineering standard for health software and health IT systems. The standard defines security activities throughout the software lifecycle, complementing IEC 62304 for development process. It is referenced by FDA, MDCG, Health Canada, and TGA as the appropriate technical standard for demonstrating security engineering rigor. Manufacturers should integrate IEC 81001-5-1 into their QMS alongside IEC 62304 as the combined standard for SaMD development.
Impact: High — Defines the cybersecurity engineering lifecycle for all health software globally
→ IEC 81001-5-1
EU / MDCG SaMD Guidance Oct 2021
MDCG 2021-24: Definitive Guidance on MDSW Qualification and Classification Under MDR
MDCG published its comprehensive guidance on the qualification and classification of Medical Device Software (MDSW) under EU MDR and IVDR. The guidance establishes the two-step qualification process: first determining whether software qualifies as MDSW (medical purpose + creates/modifies information), then classifying under MDR Rule 11 or IVDR rules. The guidance includes a decision tree, examples, and clarification on edge cases including AI-powered software. This remains the primary EU reference document for software classification.
Impact: Critical — Primary EU reference for all software classification decisions under MDR and IVDR
→ MDCG 2021-24
FDA SaMD Final Guidance June 2023
FDA Updates Content of Premarket Submissions for Device Software Functions
FDA released updated final guidance on the content required in premarket submissions for device software functions, superseding the 2005 guidance. The new guidance reflects modern software development practices, AI/ML considerations, and cybersecurity integration. It defines documentation tiers based on software device risk — Level of Concern (LOC) replaced with a risk-based framework. Documentation requirements now explicitly address AI/ML, SOUP management, and cybersecurity within the software submission package.
Impact: High — All FDA software submissions must now follow updated documentation framework
→ FDA Guidance
ISO / IEC AI/ML Published Feb 2023
ISO/IEC 23894:2023 Published — Risk Management Guidance for AI Systems
ISO/IEC 23894:2023 provides guidance on how organizations can manage risks specific to AI systems, complementing ISO 14971 for medical devices. The standard addresses AI-specific risk concepts including emergent behavior, performance degradation, bias, explainability challenges, and risks from data quality issues. For medical device manufacturers, ISO/IEC 23894 provides the AI risk management framework to integrate with their existing ISO 14971 risk management processes, as referenced in MDCG 2025-6.
Impact: Moderate — Recommended integration with ISO 14971 for all AI/ML medical device risk management programs
→ ISO/IEC 23894
